Conversion

An image can undergo format conversion, such as JPG, PNG, or WebP, altering its compression and quality. Additionally, the image can be transformed into grayscale, removing color information for a monochromatic representation.

Resize

When resizing an image, you can transform it into various dimensions like 250x250, 500x500, or 1024x1024 pixels, adjusting its visual clarity and file size accordingly. This process helps optimize the image for different display or storage requirements, maintaining its aspect ratio or cropping as needed.

Rotate

Rotating an image at 90 degrees clockwise, 90 degrees anticlockwise, or 180 degrees alters its orientation, offering different perspectives or correcting alignment issues. This transformation is useful for adjusting the visual presentation of the image to suit specific requirements or preferences.

Effects

Applying blur or smoothing effects to an image can enhance its aesthetics by reducing sharpness or noise, creating a softer and more visually pleasing appearance. These techniques are commonly used in photography and graphic design to achieve desired artistic effects or improve overall image quality.

Border

Adding a border to an image can provide a distinct visual separation from its surroundings, enhancing its overall presentation and focus. Borders can be customized in terms of color, thickness, and style to complement the image's content or the intended design aesthetic.

Flip

Flipping an image horizontally or vertically can create mirrored or inverted versions, offering different perspectives or correcting orientation. This transformation is useful for adjusting the composition or appearance of the image to suit specific design or artistic requirements.

Sketch

Converting an image into a pencil sketch and color sketch involves applying artistic filters that mimic hand-drawn techniques. These transformations can give the image a unique and artistic appearance, adding depth and character to the visual representation.

Negative effect

Converting an image into a negative effect reverses the color values, turning light areas dark and vice versa. This transformation can create a striking visual impact, offering a different perspective on the original image's composition and details.

Cropping

Cropping an image allows users to select and extract a specific portion of the image, focusing on desired elements or removing unwanted areas. This customization enables users to tailor the image's composition to their preferences or specific requirements, enhancing its visual appeal and clarity.

Add text

Adding text to an image can enhance communication by providing context, explanations, or captions directly within the visual content. This integration of text and image is commonly used in graphic design, advertising, and social media to convey messages effectively and engage viewers.
